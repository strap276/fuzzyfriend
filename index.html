About Our Rescue

Who We Are

Fuzzy Friends Ferret Rescue is a nonprofit organization dedicated to saving ferrets in need. Founded in 2010, we've helped over 1,000 ferrets find their forever homes.

Our team of experienced volunteers provides medical care, socialization, and love to each ferret that comes through our doors. We believe every ferret deserves a chance at a happy life.

All our ferrets are spayed/neutered, microchipped, and up-to-date on vaccinations before adoption. We also provide new owners with education and support to ensure a smooth transition.

Our Adoption Process

1

Online Adoption Application

We'll start by sending you a questionnaire portal to get to know you and understand your lifestyle, so we can find the perfect ferret match for you.

2

Adoption Contract

Once you've select the one that's perfect for you, you'll sign an adoption contract to make you the legal owner. This contract outlines the responsibilities and well-being of the ferret for both parties.

3

Reservation and Delivery

After signing the adoption contract, you'll make the reservation for the ferret's delivery. We'll work with you to schedule a convenient time and method for the ferret to be delivered to your home, whether that's through a direct flight, halfway drive, or delivery.

4

Pre-Delivery Preparation

We'll provide you with a starter kit with essential supplies to help you get started with your new ferret, such as food, litter, and toys. Make sure your home is ferret-proofed and ready for the new addition.

5

Meet Your New Ferret!

Finally, you'll get to meet your new furry friend! We'll ensure a smooth transition and provide guidance on caring for your ferret. Enjoy your new companion!

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the average lifespan of a ferret?

Domestic ferrets typically live 6-10 years. Proper diet, veterinary care, and living conditions can help maximize their lifespan. Our senior ferrets (5+ years) often still have several good years left and make wonderful companions.

Are ferrets good with children?

Ferrets can do well with older, gentle children (8+ years) who understand how to handle them properly. They're not recommended for homes with very young children as ferrets can be fragile and may nip if frightened. We can assess your family situation during the application process.

How much time do ferrets require daily?

Ferrets need a minimum of 4 hours of supervised playtime outside their cage each day. They thrive on interaction and mental stimulation. Many adopters choose to have two ferrets as they keep each other company, though this doubles the cleaning responsibilities.

What supplies do I need before adopting?

Essential supplies include:

  • A multi-level ferret cage (minimum 24" x 24" x 18")
  • Quality ferret food (we'll provide recommendations)
  • Litter boxes and ferret-safe litter
  • Sleeping sacks/hammocks
  • Food and water dishes
  • Toys (no rubber or foam that can be ingested)
We provide a complete checklist to approved adopters.

Can I adopt if I have other pets?

It depends on the pets. Ferrets can sometimes coexist with cats and calm dogs, but should never be left unsupervised with them. They should never be housed near birds, rodents, or reptiles - their natural prey instincts may emerge. We evaluate each situation individually during the adoption process.
